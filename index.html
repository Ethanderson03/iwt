The Science

How Thermoselect Gasification Works

A closed-loop process combining pyrolysis and high-temperature gasification to convert 100% of waste into energy and recyclable products.

1

Waste Reception & Compression

Unsorted waste enters an enclosed tipping pit with negative pressure odor control. A hydraulic press compacts waste into dense plugs, removing air pockets and evenly distributing moisture content.

MSW Industrial Waste E-Waste Sludges Tires
2

Pyrolysis (Degasification)

Waste plugs enter the degasification channel where radiant heat thermally decomposes organic matter without oxygen. This pyrolysis process breaks down waste into steam, carbon char, and preliminary synthesis gas.

Key: No combustion occurs. Pyrolysis uses only thermal energy, preventing harmful emissions.
3

High-Temperature Gasification

Carbon char enters the high-temperature chamber where controlled amounts of 95% pure oxygen transform remaining material into clean synthesis gas. Glass and metals melt for collection.

4

Shock Quench & Material Recovery

Molten material undergoes rapid shock quenching, cooling from 1,200°C to below 70°C in under one second. This produces vitrified aggregate material and recoverable metal pellets.

Output: Sand-like vitrified aggregate (for construction) + pure metal pellets for resale. Zero ash, zero landfill.
5

Gas & Water Cleaning

Synthesis gas passes through a multi-stage cleaning system that removes particulates and sulfur. The closed-loop water system extracts metals as zinc concentrate and separates chlorides as industrial-grade salts.

6

Syngas Conversion

Clean synthesis gas is converted to electricity via gas turbines, or processed through LanzaTech's gas-to-liquid technology to produce sustainable fuels.
